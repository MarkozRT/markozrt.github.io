Image registration should minimise SSD (sum of square differences). NB two identical images that align should have SSD = 0. Registration should maximize NCC (Normalized cross-correlation); two aligned images with a linear relationship between intensities have NCC = 1. Registration should maximise MI (mutual information) as well. MI is suitable for images with different modalities. A global minimum point will be shown in very dark blue whilst a global maximum point will be shown in an area of very dark red. The only similarity measure that shows a clear point is MI which has a very clear, dark red area in the top left hand corner of the second subplot. You can see the maximum point at roughly [34,30]. With NCC no clear maximum is shown and with SSD there is no clear region for a minimum shown either. The range of blue in SSD is too large and the range of dark red is too large in NCC.

The minimum value of SSD is 3.0185e+03, the maximum value for NCC is 0.4597 and the maximum value for MI is 2.6461. The corresponding translation parameters for SSD, MI and NCC are: [SSDminy1, SSDminx1] = [84,101], [MImaxy2, MImaxx2] = [30,30] and [NCCmaxy3, NCCmaxx3] = [48,8] .
